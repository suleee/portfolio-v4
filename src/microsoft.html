Overview

One day, I discovered that my friend was using Microsoft Teams, while I use Slack daily and had no prior knowledge of Microsoft Teams. My friend likes the software but occasionally gets frustrated while using it. This sparked my curiosity, and I decided to write a case study to explore the differences between the two platforms.

Problems

I began researching Microsoft Teams and discovered that it is a powerful tool often utilized by large companies and those who frequently use Microsoft software. However, I encountered some issues with the software. Many people mention that some features and options are not always intuitive or easy to use. Additionally, the product lacks in notifications

Goals

Often, big companies have very strict guidelines for design principles because they are closely tied to their brand name and goals. Furthermore, altering the design entails involving multiple teams and can be costly. In this case study, I am going to focus more on the UX than the UI aspects, as I believe that minor UX improvements can significantly enhance usability for people. The primary goal is to identify user pain points and provide solutions for information architecture.

Research Index

  • Product Research
    • What is the company selling? Understanding the core offerings and products/services of the company.
    • Who is their target audience? Identifying the specific demographic or customer groups the company aims to reach.
    • Platform usage: Check which platforms the users mainly use for the product.
  • Detecting Users' Pain Points
    • Interview people who use the product daily.
    • Find real comments on the internet to identify crucial pain points for users.
  • Conduct a comparative analysis
    • Researching what other companies do and creating a SWOT analysis.
  • Test the product
    • Use the product and try to understand the pain points. (Currently, my company works closely with Microsoft, so I have access to their applications.)
    • Use the friend's account to test it.

Detect user's pain points

In the game industry, predicting user behavior can be challenging, even after conducting numerous user playtests. Sometimes, internet user reviews play a significant role in improving the product, as they provide genuine insights from users who interact with the product daily. Whenever I launch a game, I like to read comments online to understand what aspects users find uninteresting and what bugs they encounter. I use this method to gather user feedback.

Many users have mentioned that the game is difficult to learn, some features lack intuitiveness, notifications are lacking, and there are bugs. The majority of users fall within the 35-54 age group, so I believe it's crucial to make the product more intuitive and user-friendly.

Research competitor

Microsoft Teams primarily competes with a similar service, Slack. I've also added more products to the chart to compare the target audience, goals, strengths, and unique qualities of other big companies.

Testing

At my current company, we work closely with Microsoft, and some parts of our work involve using Microsoft applications. So, I downloaded the web application to test it. However, my version lacked real data, so I borrowed my friend's computer to test specific aspects with real data. After collecting this information, I allocated time to thoroughly test the product and understand the pain points mentioned by users.

Solution

In this Case Study, I won't cover everything I found, so I'll focus on one scenario and provide a solution for it. I've chosen to address a frequent concern that many people have raised: notifications. I've identified areas where notifications are needed when actions are completed.

Some users also complained about excessive updates, so I brainstormed ways to make minor changes with a significant impact. I decided to re-evaluate the process of creating new team channels, as this is where teams communicate and share messages, tools, and files. Since the majority of users use Android and desktop, I conducted testing on these two platforms for the redesign.
