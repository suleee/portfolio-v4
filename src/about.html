Hello, I'm Jisu,

a UX/UI designer experienced in front-end development, focused on creating design systems for better digital experiences.

I studied Digital Interactive Art at Emily Carr University. I began my career as a web product designer in the marketing department. My tasks involved delivering web-related products such as websites, marketing ads, event pages, and more. During my tenure as a web designer, I consistently focused on enhancing user engagement with the products I created, guiding them toward a more immersive and enjoyable gaming experience.

To enhance my UX design skills and communication abilities, I learned programming and marketing. After taking some courses, I built two web applications during my free time and also completed some projects as a freelancer. Now, I am working as a UX/UI designer in the game industry.

I enjoy solving problems across various industries.

My Design Principles

Strong conviction

Displaying numbers is essential to support the design and convince stakeholders; therefore, I gather data that can bolster my design, such as competitor's profit figures, user conversion rates, user reviews/feedback, and more.

Thoughtful Complexity

I enjoy delving into complex challenges that require deep product thinking. I often gather ideas from unrelated products, so I consistently keep an eye on every app or web product I use. Additionally, collaborating with others helps me solve complex problems.

Process Oriented

After transitioning to a UX/UI designer role, I shifted my focus to becoming more process-oriented. I closely collaborate with my teammates to establish processes that benefit both our teams and users. This involves maintaining flexibility and adaptability to adjust priorities and outcomes as needed

Favorite books

To gain a deeper understanding, I consistently seek new knowledge. The following books were especially useful and interesting: 'The Design of Everyday Things,' 'What Silicon Valley Does and Develops' by Chan Han, '1-10 Steps of UX and Product Planning Guide,' and 'Sell or Be Sold.' One of my advantages is that I can access valuable UX/UI books written in both Korean and English. This helps me stay informed about UX trends in various contexts.
